# Axiom: Energetic Output (The Mandate of Telemetry)

## 1. Definition of System Energy
A system $S$ composed of primitives $\{p_1, p_2, ..., p_n\}$ is not merely a static arrangement of parts. To be considered an *Active System*, it must perform work. The physical manifestation of this work in our digital substrate is **Energy Emission** ($E$).

Energy is defined as the set of verifiable observations ($O$) and events ($V$) generated by the system over time interval $T$:
$$ E(S, T) = \{ O_{t \in T} \} \cup \{ V_{t \in T} \} $$

## 2. Power Output $P(S)$
The vitality of a system is measured by its Power Output ($P(S)$), which is the rate of energy emission:
$$ P(S) = \frac{\Delta \text{Events}}{\Delta t} + \frac{\Delta \text{Metrics}}{\Delta t} $$

### 2.1 The Threshold of Inertia
A system is functional if and only if $P(S) > 0$. 
If $P(S) = 0$ for a period $T > T_{stagnation}$, the system is declared **Inert**.
- **Inert State**: An inert system has collapsed into a "vacuum" of information. It is no longer in conversation with the rest of the universe.
- **Remediation**: An inert system must be re-seeded or archived.

## 3. Propagation and Consumption
The energy emitted by a micro-system is consumed by the macro-system governance layer to track status, identify drift, and trigger recursive adaptations. No system is a black box; the registry must see the "vibrancy" of its internals to maintain the holarchy.
