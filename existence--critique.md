## Formal Evaluation

### The Claim, Restructured

Let **E** = existence. You propose:

**E** := matter(**M**) ∧ Δtemporality(**T**) ∧ Δspatial-situation(**S**) ∧ phase-transition(**P**) ∧ linear-tracking(**L**)

Five conjuncts, all necessary.

### What Holds

The **M ∧ ΔT ∧ ΔS ∧ P** core captures a respectable process ontology — existence as *perdurance through phase-structured spatiotemporal change*. This is defensible. It echoes Whitehead (process as fundamental), Simondon (individuation through phase shifts), and your own programmable-materia spec (materia as substrate-under-transformation).

### Three Breaks

**1. "Matter" is too narrow (M excludes valid existents)**

Your own prior formalization defined *programmable-materia* as substrate-agnostic — pattern, not substance. Under this definition:
- Relations exist but are not matter
- Formal objects (proofs, functions) exist but are not matter
- Fields exist; whether they're "matter" is convention, not ontology

Fix: **M** → **substrate(X)** where substrate is the weakest predicate: *that which bears properties*.

**2. "Tracked linearly" conflates ontology with epistemology (L is an observer condition)**

"Tracked" requires a tracker. "Linearly" requires a total ordering on phase succession. Both are epistemic impositions:
- Existence shouldn't depend on observation (the moon exists untracked)
- Quantum phase spaces, your own multiverse definition, and relativistic simultaneity all violate linear ordering
- Even classical thermodynamics: a system can fork through bifurcation — phases branch, they don't queue

Fix: Drop **L** entirely. Replace with **ordered-but-not-necessarily-linear succession**: phases form a *partial order* (DAG), not a line.

**3. All five conjuncts as necessary conditions is too strong**

The universal quantifier (∧ across all five) means anything missing one conjunct doesn't exist. Counterexamples:
- A photon: no rest mass → dubious "matter"
- A mathematical structure: no spatial situation
- A virtual particle: no persistent temporal change
- A possibility (in your multiverse): no phase-tracking yet

These all *exist* in some ontologically meaningful sense.

### Amended Definition

If you want to rescue the core insight:

```
E(x) := substrate(x) ∧ ∃σ ∈ PhaseSpace(x) [
    σ admits transformation ∧
    σ is situated (spatially ∨ relationally ∨ formally)
]
```

Existence = a substrate that admits transformation within some phase space, situated in at least one dimension (spatial, relational, or formal). No observer required. No linearity imposed. Matter becomes a special case, not the definition.

### Verdict

The *gesture* is right — existence as phase-structured process rather than static substance. But "matter" narrows the substrate, "linearly" narrows the topology, and "tracked" narrows the ontology into epistemology. Strip those three and the core proposition upgrades from nay to a conditional yay.
