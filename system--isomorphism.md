# Axiom: Isomorphisic Validation (The Escrow Threshold)

## 1. Definition of Isomorphism $\Psi$
The **Isomorphism Test** $\Psi(\alpha, R)$ is the criteria for transitioning an abstraction $\alpha$ from a theoretical state into the physical registry $R$ as an operational node.

An abstraction $\alpha$ is isomorphic if there exists a bijective mapping $f: \alpha \to R$ such that for every structural property $p \in \alpha$, $f(p)$ matches the empirical truth state exhibited in the workspace.

## 2. Truthful Positioning
"Containers must prove themselves as the isomorphisms of reality... that's when they can open up and interact with the system."

A structure $\alpha$ is held in **Escrow** precisely because it has not yet proven its truthful positioning. To "open up" means to establish functional relationship edges with other **Active** nodes.
- **$\Psi(\alpha)$ is True** iff:
  - 1. **Mathematical Consistency**: Internal logic does not violate any foundational `LAWS.md`.
  - 2. **Empirical Verifiability**: Every claim made by $\alpha$ is reflected in the active file-system or agent-state.
  - 3. **Formal Grounding**: All referenced entities $E \in \alpha$ must themselves be Active or Formalized.

## 3. The Adjudication Threshold
No entity may influence the Triptych operational repositories (Body, Mind, Seed) unless it passes $\Psi$. Any attempt to forcibly project a non-isomorphic structure into the active substrate violates the Law of Isomorphism and will result in either a rejected transaction or a "hallucination vacuum" state.
- **The Adjudicator**: The process of proving isomorphism is a shared responsibility between the **Formal Agent** (verification) and the **System Owner** (adjudication).
